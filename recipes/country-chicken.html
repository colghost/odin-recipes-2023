Easy Air-Fryer Chicken Parmesan

Description

If going to the Cracker Barrell is too much work, this might be perfect.

I'm hungry just thinking about it.

Ingredients

  • 2 large chicken breasts, halved
  • a whole bunch of vegetable oil for frying
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1/2 heavy whipping cream
  • 1 pint buttermilk
  • 3 eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 tablespoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • salt and pepper.

Steps

  1. After cutting chicken, put it in freezer bag and dump buttermilk over it. Refrigerate for one hour prior to cooking.
  2. In a large pot, heat cooking oil to about 350 degrees. You want atleast 2 inches of oil in the bottom of the pan
  3. Mix together flour and all the other dry ingredients in a shallow dish or on a plate until fully combined. Immediately remove about a quarter cup of the flour mixture and set aside.
  4. In another shallow dish, mix together your eggs and milk. Whisk it well.
  5. Dredge your chicken through the flour mixture, then through the milk/egg mixture, then back through the flour mix again
  6. Toss those birds in the preheated oil. Don't crowd the pan. You'll regret it.
  7. Cook each piece for about three minuites on each side. Remove to a paper towl-lined plate or a rakc over paper towels to drain the grease.
  8. Drain just about all the oil. Leave enough in teh bottom to make a roux and keep the "drippings" in the bottom.
  9. Return the pan to the heat, add in the flour mixture, and stir it to combine with the grease.
  10. Once the gravy is gravied, whisk in your chicken stock. Once that comes to a simmer, dump in heavy whipping cream. Bring back to a simmer. Get it thick.
  11. Remove gravy from heat and let it cool for about five minutes. Plate your chicken and serve with gravy.
